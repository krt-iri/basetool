## 11. How to Sign

You sign this Agreement once. The signature covers all of Your present
and future Contributions to the Project unless and until You explicitly
withdraw under [§ 12 — Withdrawal](#12-withdrawal).

### Primary signing path (signature PR)

1. Open a pull request titled `cla: sign — <your-github-handle>`
   against [`docs/cla-signatures.md`](docs/cla-signatures.md), adding a
   new row with:
   - Your full legal name.
   - Your GitHub handle.
   - The email address You use for Git commits.
   - The date in ISO-8601 format (`YYYY-MM-DD`).
   - The CLA version You are signing (currently `1.0`).
2. Include the following sign-off in the pull-request description,
   **verbatim**:

   > I have read the IRIDIUM Basetool Individual Contributor License
   > Agreement, version 1.0, and I hereby accept and agree to its terms
   > for all of my present and future Contributions to the Project.

3. Wait for a Project Maintainer to merge the signature PR before
   opening Your first Contribution PR. The maintainer will verify that
   the email and name on the signature PR match the commits You intend
   to submit.

### Alternative: CLA-Assistant

The Project Maintainers may set up
[CLA-Assistant](https://cla-assistant.io/) so contributors sign
electronically by adding a single comment to their first PR. If
CLA-Assistant is active on the repository, follow its in-PR
instructions and skip the signature-PR procedure above. The agreement
You sign electronically is the same one You see in this file.

### Entity (corporate) contributors

If You make Contributions on behalf of Your employer or another legal
entity, the entity must execute a separate **Entity CLA**. Contact
[lucas.greuloch@pm.me](mailto:lucas.greuloch@pm.me) to obtain the
Entity CLA template. Until the Entity CLA is countersigned, Your
employer-funded Contributions cannot be merged.

### What a maintainer checks before merging Your first PR

- Your GitHub handle appears in [`docs/cla-signatures.md`](docs/cla-signatures.md) (or has been recorded by CLA-Assistant).
- The author email on every commit in the PR matches the email You signed with.
- Every commit carries a `Signed-off-by:` trailer per the
  [DCO sign-off requirement](CONTRIBUTING.md#developer-certificate-of-origin-dco-sign-off)
  in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
